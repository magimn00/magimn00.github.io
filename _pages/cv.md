# Magzhan Mukanova

Ph.D. student in School of Applied and Creative Computing at Purdue University researching embodied virtual agents, behavioral animation, personality expression, and human-computer interaction.

My work focuses on how virtual humans communicate personality and social behavior through subtle facial, body, and gesture motion. I develop MetaHuman-based conversational agents, procedural animation systems, and perception studies investigating familiarity, trust, and behavioral appearance.
